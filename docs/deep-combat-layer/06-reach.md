## Decision: full GURPS reach identity (option c)

A weapon's **range becomes its reach** in the GURPS sense — not just "how far it can target" but a
full spatial identity with strengths *and* a real weakness. Reach-2 weapons are spears, poles, and
polearms; reach-1 is swords, axes, maces; reach-0 / point-blank is fists and the like.

## The reach-2 identity

A reach-2 weapon (spear/pole/polearm) gets a coherent **space-control / safe-poke** identity:

1. **Outrange.** It can strike a reach-1 foe from 2 tiles — a tile the reach-1 weapon can't strike
   back from.
2. **Escape-counter.** Because it can hit from outside reach-1 range, it **avoids the counter**: a
   reach-1 defender can't counter-attack a blow delivered from 2 tiles away. Poking from range is
   genuinely safer.

This is the lancer/dragoon fantasy: control space, strike from safety, deny the counter.

## The reach-2 weakness: point-blank

To keep reach-2 from being a strict upgrade, it has a real downside: a reach-2 weapon is
**penalized when fighting a foe who is *adjacent* (inside its reach).** A long polearm is clumsy at
grappling distance. So:

- The **counter to a spearman is to rush inside its reach** — close the gap and it's fighting at a
  penalty.
- This gives short weapons a reason to exist against long ones, and turns the spacing itself into a
  duel: the spear wants to keep you at 2, you want to get to 1.

## Stop-hit as an ability, not a universal rule

GURPS lets a longer weapon get a free strike on someone closing in. In the DCL this is a
**lancer-class ability** (an earned, equippable/learnable thing), **not** a universal rule that
every long weapon has. Reasons:

- A universal free attack-of-opportunity on approach would make rushing the spearman (the intended
  counter) too costly and break the point-blank counterplay above.
- As an ability, it becomes a build choice and a class identity — "this lancer punishes
  approachers" — rather than baseline friction.

So: **stop-hit** = a free strike on an enemy entering the weapon's reach, granted to specific
lancer abilities only.

## Why this matters

Reach turns the grid into a real spacing game that meshes with the rest of the DCL:

- It adds a fourth spatial lever alongside the counterplay triangle (`05`): you can beat a defended
  foe by *outranging* it as well as flanking / focus-firing / crushing it.
- It gives ranged-ish melee (polearms) a distinct role between true ranged (`03`, missiles) and true
  melee (swords), so the weapon families don't collapse into "melee vs ranged".

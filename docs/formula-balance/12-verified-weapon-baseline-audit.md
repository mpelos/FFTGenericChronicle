## Interpretation

The verified baseline changes the problem from "missing weapon data" to "raw vanilla max WP is not
a safe direct input for the v0.2 design policy."

The code-mod direction is still valid. In fact, this audit strengthens the need for a flexible
runtime formula layer:

- weapon-family damage can no longer be balanced by assuming vanilla WP scale is already healthy;
- premium outliers such as max knight swords, spears, and guns need explicit engine-aware control;
- weak-family buffs such as staff, rod, book, crossbow, and axe cannot be represented by vanilla
  WP maxima alone;
- the armor response model needs to be retuned with verified equipment tiers, not simply applied
  to raw endgame maxima.

## V1 Requirements

Before a policy can be promoted beyond v0.2:

1. Choose a verified equipment sampling policy: max WP, non-rare max WP, shop-end WP, phase-tier
   WP, or explicit modded WP.
2. Produce `work/sim-inputs-v1.json` from verified IVC weapon data and the chosen sampling policy.
3. Re-run `tools/sim_damage.py --scorecard`.
4. Re-establish the five scorecard gates from `11-validated-policy-v0.2.md`.
5. Decide which outliers are handled by formula scaling, support-engine caps, modded weapon WP,
   or family-specific action rules.

Until then, v0.2 remains:

```text
Conceptually viable, superseded for weapon-WP proof by verified-baseline audit.
```

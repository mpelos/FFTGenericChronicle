# Squire (Vanilla / IVC)

*Classic: Squire · Command: Fundaments · Stats (mult.): HP 100 · MP 75 · Speed 100 · PA 90 · MA 80 · Move 4 · Jump 3 · C-Ev 5%*

The entry generic job. Every recruit starts here, and it is the gateway every other generic job
unlocks from. Its own kit is deliberately shallow — Squire exists to be left behind.

## End-game tier: D

**Community read.** Squire is universally seen as a starter and a stepping-stone, not a job you
field late. Its lasting reputation is built on a handful of *portable* support abilities (JP grind,
mobility) rather than on its command. (Ramza's special Squire is a different, far stronger class —
see `21-ramza.md`; do not confuse the two.)

**Why this tier.** As a fielded primary at end-game it has no scaling offense, no magic, no
equipment identity, and a command that every specialized job outperforms. Its balanced stat line is
the "100% baseline" the other jobs are measured against, which by definition means it wins nowhere.

## Strengths

- **Move 4 baseline** — unusually mobile for a tier-0 job; the chassis feels nimble early.
- **Cheap, broad portable kit** — its supports (JP grind, Move +1, axe access) and Counter Tackle
  splash onto *any* build, so the job pays for itself indirectly long after you stop fielding it.
- **No prerequisites** — free access to the whole skill economy from turn one.

## Weaknesses

- **Fundaments is thin and low-impact** — self-buff is slow, the damage skills are random chip.
- **PA 90 / MA 80** — below-baseline on both offense axes; no role it does better than a specialist.
- **No innate ability, no equipment niche** — nothing that survives into the late game.

## Action skills — Fundaments

| Skill | Effect | Design read |
|------|--------|-------------|
| Focus | Raise own Physical Attack | Costs a whole turn for a buff that is usually worse than just attacking; only matters in slow setup/cheese. |
| Rush | Random physical dash damage | Cheap chip with a small repositioning/knock element; unreliable. |
| Throw Stone | Ranged random rock damage | Trivial damage; real use is a free ranged poke (finish a crystal, trigger a tile, ping a flier). |
| Salve | Clear negative statuses (area) | The one keeper — cheap, MP-free field cleanse before you have items or White Magick. |

## Reaction / Support / Movement (cross-job value)

This is where Squire actually earns its keep — the abilities in its orbit are cheap and travel onto
every build. (IVC pins no innate R/S/M on Squire; the kit below is the job's classic portable set.)

- **Reaction — Counter Tackle:** physical counter on being hit. Cheap, fine on a frontliner, but
  strictly weaker than Counter/Hamedo-class reactions; a budget pick.
- **Support — Gained JP-UP (≈ JP Boost):** more JP earned. Snowballs the entire ability economy and
  is close to an auto-include while building — its near-mandatory status is a known balance wart.
- **Support — Gained EXP-UP (≈ EXP Boost):** more EXP. Situational, because deliberate *level
  control* matters in FFT (enemy levels scale); useful for fast growth, a liability for low-level runs.
- **Support — Equip Axe:** axes on any job — high random-damage ceiling, niche.
- **Movement — Move +1:** +1 move for cheap; broadly useful mobility that fits almost any build.

## Build relevance

A stepping-stone, never a destination. The end-game play is to mine Squire's portable supports
(JP grind early, Move +1 forever) and field a specialist instead. Its command does not anchor any
serious build.

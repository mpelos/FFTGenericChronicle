# Chemist (Vanilla / IVC)

*Classic: Chemist · Command: Items · Innate: Throw Items · Stats (mult.): HP 80 · MP 75 · Speed 100 · PA 75 · MA 80 · Move 3 · Jump 3 · C-Ev 5%*

The item user: healing, revival, and status-cure that cost no MP and ignore Faith. The other half
of the generic starting pair, and the backbone of reliable sustain all game long.

## End-game tier: B  (as a portable kit: S)

**Community read.** The *Items* command and Auto-Potion are regarded as some of the most reliable
tools in the game — they work when magic cannot (immune to Silence, low Faith, MP denial) and they
revive cheaply. The Chemist *job itself* is fragile and does zero offense, so it is prized as a
secondary/support splash far more than as a fielded primary.

**Why this tier.** Reliability without scaling. Fixed-value items keep a team alive against anything,
but they do not grow with stats, so a pure Chemist contributes nothing but triage by end-game. As a
portable kit grafted onto a durable job, that triage is best-in-class — hence the split rating.

## Strengths

- **Faith- and MP-independent healing/revive** — the most *reliable* sustain in the game; unaffected
  by Silence, Atheist/low-Faith, or MP burn.
- **Phoenix Down** — cheap, instant revive at range (with Throw Items); turns a wipe into a recovery.
- **Portable status cures** — Remedy and friends are a walking cleanse for the whole party.

## Weaknesses

- **Squishy and offenceless** — HP 80, PA 75, Move 3; no combat presence of its own.
- **Items do not scale** — fixed numbers fall behind big late-game HP pools unless you spend heavily
  on X-Potion/Elixir.
- **Purely reactive** — keeps the team alive but never wins the fight itself.

## Action skills — Items

| Skill | Effect | Design read |
|------|--------|-------------|
| Potion / High Potion / X-Potion | Restore small / medium / large HP | The healing spine; X-Potion is the workhorse top-end heal. |
| Ether / High Ether | Restore MP | Keeps casters going; mostly luxury given JP cost. |
| Elixir | Full HP + MP | Powerful but very expensive JP; a clutch button, not a rotation. |
| Antidote / Eye Drops / Echo Herbs / Maiden's Kiss / Gold Needle / Holy Water / Remedy | Cure specific statuses (Poison / Blind / Silence / Toad / Petrify / undead etc.); Remedy clears many | Faith-proof cleanse; Remedy collapses the toolkit into one slot once afforded. |
| Phoenix Down | Revive (random HP; damages undead) | Cheapest revive in the game and the reason Items is on so many bars. |

## Reaction / Support / Movement (cross-job value)

Chemist's portable tools are top-shelf. (IVC makes **Throw Items** innate to the job.)

- **Support — Throw Items (innate):** use any item at range. Converts every single-target item into
  a ranged heal/revive, which is what makes a back-line medic build work. Best-in-slot support.
- **Reaction — Auto-Potion:** auto-consume a potion when hit. Cheap, automatic self-sustain on any
  frontliner; one of the most-used reactions in the game.
- **Movement — Move-Find Item (≈ Treasure Hunter):** pick up hidden tile loot. Pure farming utility,
  no combat value.

## Build relevance

The premier *support secondary*. The standard pattern is a durable physical job running **Items** as
its secondary plus **Auto-Potion**, making it self-healing without a dedicated medic. As a primary,
only worth it for a committed back-line healer. Low standalone-job value, enormous splash value.

# Mystic (Vanilla / IVC)

*Classic: Oracle · Command: Mystic Arts (Yin-Yang) · Stats (mult.): HP 75 · MP 110 · Speed 100 · PA 50 · MA 120 · Move 3 · Jump 3 · C-Ev 5%*

The status caster: a broad library of debuffs and control effects, plus Faith/Brave manipulation and
HP/MP drain. Powerful on paper, unreliable in practice.

## End-game tier: C  (mainstream view leans D)

**Community read.** Mystic/Oracle is the classic "great kit, bad hit rates" job. It can blind,
silence, sleep, confuse, petrify, and bend Faith/Brave — but the infliction is MA/Faith/zodiac-gated
and *misses a lot*, and late enemies are often status-immune. It does almost no direct damage. The
ceiling (disable a key threat, sink an enemy's Faith, drain a caster dry) is real; the floor (a whiffed
turn) is what defines its reputation.

**Why this tier.** Its control matters when it lands, but unreliability and zero damage keep it out of
the front rank; it is a situational specialist, not a staple.

## Strengths

- **Broad control library:** disables, silence (anti-caster), sleep, confuse, and hard removals
  (petrify/transform) — answers to many specific threats in one command.
- **Faith / Brave manipulation:** Belief/Disbelief and Trepidation bend the magic and physical
  exchange — including raising an enemy's Faith so *your* nukers hit harder.
- **HP/MP drain:** Invigoration (damage + self-heal) and Empowerment (MP denial) give it sustain and
  caster-starving utility.

## Weaknesses

- **Low, multi-gated success rates** — MA, Faith, and zodiac all tax the infliction; the kit whiffs
  often, and status immunity shuts it off entirely against bosses.
- **No real damage; fragile** — HP 75, C-Ev 5%; it can't pressure or protect itself.
- **Turn-expensive control** — a slow way to apply effects faster jobs make irrelevant.

## Action skills — Mystic Arts

> Sources tag most of these only as "inflict a status" without the exact status, so the table groups
> them by role rather than pinning each effect.

| Skill | Effect | Design read |
|------|--------|-------------|
| Umbra / Quiescence / Fervor / Delirium / Hesitation / Repose / Induration | Inflict a status (blind/silence/sleep/confuse/disable/petrify family) | The control spread; high ceiling, success-gated and often resisted. |
| Belief / Disbelief | Raise / lower target Faith | Permanent magic-exchange lever (set up your nukers, or sink theirs). |
| Trepidation | Lower target Bravery | Cuts an enemy's physical damage and reaction rate. |
| Empowerment / Invigoration | Drain MP / drain HP (heal self) | Resource denial and self-sustain. |
| Corruption | Inflict status; undead interaction | Status pressure with an undead angle. |
| Harmony | Clear status(es) | A cleanse option inside an otherwise offensive kit. |
| Chant | Special-case behavior | Niche; not a workhorse. |

## Reaction / Support / Movement (cross-job value)

Mystic's exports are thin; its value is the command, not portable slots.

- **Support — caster economy / MA scaling:** like any MA job it wants cheaper casts and more magic
  power, but it brings no signature support of its own.
- **Reaction / Movement:** nothing defining — Mystic is fielded for its status command, not mined for
  splash abilities.

## Build relevance

A situational specialist primary: bring it to disable a specific threat, to bend a key unit's
Faith/Brave, or to drain a caster — accepting that the effects may miss. It anchors no reliable build
and exports little; its niche is control no other job offers, at the price of consistency.

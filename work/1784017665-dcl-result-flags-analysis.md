## Native contract

The high nibble of `unit+0x1E5` is a bitwise numeric-result bundle:

- `0x80`: HP debit;
- `0x40`: HP credit;
- `0x20`: MP debit;
- `0x10`: MP credit.

Native writers emit combined `0x50` (HP+MP credit) and `0x90` (HP debit+MP credit),
which proves that the field is not a single exclusive enum. The selector reads the
bundle with a fixed primary-presentation priority: HP debit first, MP debit second,
low-bit effects next, and the shared HP/MP credit route last.

A partial Mana Shield split therefore has the structural bundle `0xA0`. The shared
HP/MP apply routine consumes both staged numbers independently, and the selector chooses
HP damage as the primary presentation. There is no verified literal native `0xA0` writer;
its numeric application is Strong from the bitwise contract, while the exact popup plus
Mana Shield animation combination remains a live presentation gate.

## Runtime consequence

`DclResultFlagsControlEnabled` rebuilds only the numeric high nibble from the final four
staged channels and preserves configured low effect bits. The flag write is the last
member of the existing transactional channel write, so a failure rolls the earlier HP/MP
writes back before the callback fails open.
